# NeKI — Newsletter Knowledge Index

NeKI is a compact index to selected third-party developer writing. Entries are leads for research and implementation ideas; they are not Apple documentation, API contracts, or approved architecture decisions. NeKI publishes routing metadata, not the linked articles' text.

## Lookup

- [Sources](sources.md) — publications currently represented by NeKI.
- [Newsletters](newsletters/) — curated editions that link to selected external reading.
- [Blogs](blogs/) — complete catalogues of each source's own articles or long-form posts.
- [Swift](topics/swift.md), [SwiftUI](topics/swiftui.md), [UIKit](topics/uikit.md), [SwiftData](topics/swiftdata.md), [Core Data](topics/core-data.md), [Liquid Glass](topics/liquid-glass.md), and [Xcode](topics/xcode.md) — contextual reading leads across both source types.
- Focused lookups: [Observation & State Management](topics/observation-state.md), [Navigation & Deep Linking](topics/navigation-deep-linking.md), [Networking](topics/networking.md), [Persistence & Synchronisation](topics/persistence-sync.md), [Swift Package Manager](topics/swift-package-manager.md), [Macros & Metaprogramming](topics/macros-metaprogramming.md), [Dependency Injection](topics/dependency-injection.md), [Composable Architecture](topics/composable-architecture.md), [App Intents](topics/app-intents.md), and [AI Development](topics/ai-development.md).
- [Candidate domains](candidate-domains.md) — frequently linked sites. Frequency is not a recommendation or quality rating.
- [Collection status](collection-status.json) — freshness, coverage, source format, public catalogue type, and the most recent additions, removals, or updated entries by source.

For an individual publication's indexed entries, use [newsletters](newsletters/) for curated editions or [blogs](blogs/) for complete article catalogues. Every newsletter issue links to its original page and contains source, date, topic, visible-section, and selected external editorial links with concise NeKI-authored briefs. Blog entries receive a brief for the indexed article itself; topic files separate direct-source reading from newsletter leads.
